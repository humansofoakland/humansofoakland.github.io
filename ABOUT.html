"Inspired by Brandon Stanten's Humans of New York, I created Humans of Oakland to capture and showcase my hometown. I have walked the streets of Oakland for sixteen years and always wondered about peopleâ€™s lives - their celebrations, downfalls, inspirations. In my free time, I roam around taking street portraits, conversing with people, and sharing their stories and portraits online. Art thrives on the unexpected, just like the conversations and connections I make with the people I interview. That art is the culture of positivity and hope. It is the breaking down of internal biases that many of us possess in our community through a process of understanding each other. Each person is special and unique not just in the amount of melanin in their skin, but through their lived experiences. The personal relationships I have made evoke compassion within me. This too is what I hope will happen to people reading the stories I publish."
